Project Overview

Converting domestic waste heat into accessible renewable energy.

⚡
ABSTRACT

The primary objective is the design of a prototype capable of transforming heat into electrical energy.

By utilizing the Seebeck effect via thermoelectric (Peltier) modules, this system successfully established a temperature difference of over 150°C, generating significant electrical output from recycled thermal waste.

🔥

The Challenge

Egypt faces rising energy demands while combatting climate change. A massive portion of energy in domestic and industrial sectors is lost as Waste Heat. This remains an unutilized, untapped resource that causes environmental harm and reduces overall system efficiency.

🌎

The Solution

A highly scalable, eco-friendly heat recovery system. Simulating a domestic iron as a heat source and using recycled materials for cooling, the prototype directly converts thermal differentials into clean electricity, exceeding design goals by producing up to 2700 Joules.

System Architecture

Select a component to explore its role in the thermal energy conversion process.

🔥

Electric Iron

Acting as the primary heat source to simulate domestic waste heat. The iron successfully reaches and maintains extreme temperatures of approximately 180°C to 200°C.

❄

Recycled Fan + Ice/Salt

A recycled computer laptop fan coupled with a mixture of ice and salt. This drives the cold-side temperature down to 0°C, ensuring the maximum possible thermal gradient.

⚡

Thermoelectric Generators (TEG)

The core of the system. Positioned between the hot and cold extremes, these modules utilize the Seebeck Effect to generate voltage directly from the temperature differential.

🧱

Aluminum & Compound

Aluminum plates distribute intense heat evenly to prevent module damage. Thermal compound (paste) bridges microscopic gaps, drastically improving heat transfer efficiency.

🔋

Battery & Circuitry

The generated current is regulated using a 1Ω to 10Ω resistor and routed to a rechargeable lithium battery, allowing the captured energy to be stored and utilized practically.

Mathematical Analysis

Verification of thermal dynamics and electrical efficiency.

governing_laws.eqn
// 1. Electrical Power
P = V² / R
// 2. Total Energy (Joules)
E = P × t
// 3. Fourier's Heat Conduction
Q = (k × A × ΔT) / L
Constants Used:
R = 1 Ω
t = 300 s
Qin = 10,800 J

Trial 1 Evaluation

P = (1.8)² / 1 = 3.24 W
E = 3.24 × 300 = 972 J
Efficiency = (972 / 10800) × 100 = 9%

Trial 2 Evaluation

P = (2.0)² / 1 = 4.0 W
E = 4.0 × 300 = 1200 J
Efficiency = (1200 / 10800) × 100 = 11%

Trial 3 Evaluation (Final)

P = (3.0)² / 1 = 9.0 W
E = 9.0 × 300 = 2700 J
Efficiency = (2700 / 10800) × 100 = 25%
